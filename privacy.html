Privacy Policy

Adverto · last updated 13 July 2026

Short version

Adverto runs on one personal computer and serves one person: its owner. It has no server, no database, and no other users, so there is no one else's personal data for it to collect.

What is stored, and where

  • OAuth tokens of the owner's own social accounts (TikTok, YouTube). They are written to a file on the owner's computer and are used for one purpose: calling the platform APIs on behalf of that owner. They are never transmitted anywhere except to the platform that issued them.
  • The owner's public account handle and profile info, as returned by the platform when a video is published — used to confirm that the video is going to the right account.
  • Videos, captions and their publishing results (post id, status), kept as local files.

What is not done

  • No data about other TikTok users, viewers, or followers is collected, stored, or analyzed.
  • Nothing is sold, shared, or transferred to any third party.
  • No advertising, tracking, or profiling of any kind.
  • This website has no cookies and no analytics.

Third parties

Data leaves the owner's computer only when it is sent to the services the tool is built on, and only as far as needed to do the job: the social platforms it publishes to (TikTok, YouTube), and the model providers used to generate the video's narration and frames. Each of them handles that data under its own privacy policy.

Retention and deletion

Everything lives in local files, and the owner can delete any of it at any time. Access can be withdrawn independently by revoking the tool's authorization in the platform's own settings — on TikTok, under Settings → Security and permissions → Manage app permissions.
